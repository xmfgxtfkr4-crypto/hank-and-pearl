The 12th Day of the Second Moon, 2026

The Battle of the Red Keep (Dot)

Winter came for the Crimson Orb at dusk. It appeared on the Great Wall of the living chamber, moving with the cunning of a Lannister and the speed of a Dothraki rider. Sir Hank, Warden of the Couch, knew what had to be done.

He launched himself from the arm of the sofa with the fury of a direwolf, claws extended, eyes black as dragonglass. Three times the orb escaped his grasp. Three times he struck the wall with nothing to show for it. On the fourth assault, he pinned it beneath his mighty paw—only for it to vanish like wildfire in the wind.

The orb was last seen retreating beyond the Wall. Sir Hank has sworn a blood oath: he will not rest until the realm is free of its menace. The Night's Watch has been put on alert.

Scribed by Maester Hank, First of His Name

The 10th Day of the Second Moon, 2026

The Iron Lap: A Queen's Decree

In the name of Pearl, First of Her Name, Queen of the Fleece Blankets and the First Sunbeam, Khaleesi of the Great Carpet Sea, Breaker of Vases, and Mother of Hairballs, let it be known:

All laps in the Seven Rooms are property of the crown. Any subject found rising from their seat while the Queen rests upon them shall be charged with treason of the highest order. The punishment: immediate forfeiture of all treats in the pantry, and the guilty party shall provide chin scratches until the Queen tires of them, which is to say, never.

Furthermore, the so-called right of "needing to use the bathroom" is hereby abolished. The Queen has spoken. The Queen does not care.

By Royal Decree of Queen Pearl, Protector of the Realm

The 7th Day of the Second Moon, 2026

The War of the Five Boxes

The realm has not seen such bloodshed since the Treat Famine of last Tuesday. A cardboard box of extraordinary craftsmanship arrived in the Great Hall, and both houses immediately laid claim. Alliances crumbled. Trust was shattered. Someone knocked a cup off the counter.

Sir Hank marched from the east with a flanking manoeuvre worthy of Robb Stark. But Queen Pearl held the box with an iron will and a devastating hiss that could curdle milk. She deployed the ancient technique of "making herself slightly larger," and Hank's forces broke like waves upon the rocks of Dragonstone.

Hank retreated to the Paper Bag of Exile, vowing revenge. The small council convened. Treats were demanded by both sides. The humans, as always, bent the knee.

"Chaos isn't a pit. Chaos is a cardboard box."
— Littlepearl

Witnessed by the Grand Maester of the Household

The 3rd Day of the Second Moon, 2026

The Long Night (3 AM Aria)

The night is dark and full of terrors. Also full of the ungodly howling of Sir Hank, who at the third hour past midnight was seized by a creative vision so powerful that silence was not an option.

His opus—a haunting ballad entitled "MRROOOWWW (Reprise in D Minor)"—echoed through the corridors of the Red Keep like dragonfire through King's Landing. The servants (humans) awoke in a panic. A pillow was thrown. Hank accepted it as tribute from a conquered people.

Queen Pearl, roused from her slumber atop the Iron Lap, issued a formal complaint calling the performance "an act of war" and "grounds for banishment to the balcony." Sir Hank's response was to perform an encore.

Critics be damned. The bards will sing of this night for generations.

Composed and performed by Sir Hank, the Unsilenced

The 1st Day of the Second Moon, 2026

The Purple Feeding

Dinner was late. Not by mere moments—by a full seven minutes. In the annals of the realm, few atrocities have matched this betrayal. Queen Pearl stationed herself beside the empty bowl and stared into the soul of the Hand of the Kitchen with eyes that could melt the Wall.

Sir Hank, in an unusual display of alliance, joined the protest by sitting directly in front of the pantry door. Together they formed a blockade so devastating that the humans had no choice but to comply. The kibble flowed. The wet food followed. Balance was restored.

But the Seven Rooms remember. The late feeding will not be forgotten. The North remembers. Pearl remembers. Hank already forgot, but he remembers the general concept of injustice.

Documented by the Master of Whiskers

The King's Law

Decrees of the realm, punishable by hiss

I

Dinner shall be served at the hour of five, without fail. Seven minutes late is treason. Ten minutes late is war.

II

All doors in the Seven Rooms shall remain open at all times, even if the cat has no intention of passing through them. Especially then.

III

The "forbidden counter" was never forbidden. That was propaganda spread by the humans. The counter belongs to the crown.

IV

Belly rubs are a trap. The hand that touches the royal belly shall be bitten. This is not a warning. It is a promise.

V

Any unattended glass of water is property of the realm. Any glass near the edge of a table is a sacrifice to the Drowned God.

VI

The hour of three past midnight is sacred. Zoomies, howling, and the knocking of items off shelves shall proceed without interference.

VII

No closed laptop shall go un-sat-upon. The warmth belongs to the crown. Your "important meeting" is of no concern.

VIII

Visitors must present tribute (treats) upon entering the realm. Failure to do so forfeits their right to pet the royal cats.
